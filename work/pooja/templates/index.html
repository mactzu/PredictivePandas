What is BeerNext?

When starting out on this machine learning project we were told to 'pick something we were passionate about'. Well, that was easy - food and alcohol ... some of the greatest joys in life, right? We knew we wanted to create a recommendation engine and as we started looking around, we found plenty. Restaurant recommenders, cuisine recommenders, locality-based recommenders and of course the wine recommenders. But what about beer? Not too many out there. Whilst worldwide beer consumption is on the decline, the interest in craft beer is surging. Driven by passionate home brewers and craft beer makers, together with increasingly more sophisticated beer drinkers, the choices are growing and exponentially. So how do you find that illusive 'best' beer when you don't even know it's out there?

There no doubt that taste is subjective. There are five taste sensations: sweet, sour, salty, bitter and umami and they’re all in beer. It’s an acidic drink, typically pH 3.00–6.00 but in most there’s also some sweetness (unless it’s dry). There’s salt in the water used and bitterness, which was once used for balance is a whole genre these days. Finally, there’s that rich maltiness - the umami!

Developing your palate isn’t complicated, it just takes practice. First you start with understanding your bitterness threshold. Then it’s on to the serious business of recognising hops, malts and of course those all-important “off flavours”. There’s also a little reading and research involved, to learn about what you’re drinking. Lastly, you want buddies, the kind that know a good thing!

Whether it’s mouth open or closed (and yes, it makes a difference), this is the hard part. Take some time. You only get one shot at this before tasting and we humans can distinguish at least 10,000 distinct smells! Then start big with something like green or fruity before going for some nuance. Hone it. Is there cut grass or oregano maybe? If you know your hops, yeast and malt smells, even better.

Easy to verbalise, see and agree on is perhaps why this doesn’t tend to get a lot of time. Still, what’s that saying, we drink with our eyes? Can’t imagine too much on a hot day beating a light straw coloured, clear beer, with a tan and creamy head. Follow it up with a comment about the spidery lacing and we’ve ticked all the boxes, colour, clarity and lacing. Boom!
